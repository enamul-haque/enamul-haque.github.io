I am a Senior Research Data Scientist at the Spencer Center for Vision Research at Stanford University School of Medicine. My current research areas at Stanford include bioinformatics, data mining, NLP, and statistical learning to identify influential factors for eye disease among patients with a specific focus on research at the intersection of aging, dementia, and ocular disease. I recently joined Truveta Inc. as a Senior Data Scientist. I am broadly interested in developing methods for precision medicine by applying graph machine learning and statistical modeling at the intersection of EHR, clinical trials, genomics, and related healthcare data. I am also involved in eye care research at U.S. Department of Veterans Affairs, Palo Alto, CA.
I obtained my Ph.D. in Computer Science from the department of Computer Science at the University of Louisiana at Lafayette, LA, USA. I worked at the Network Science Research Group. (NSRG) under the supervision of Dr. Mehmet Engin Tozal. I spent one year at the Computer and Information Science department at Temple university, Philadelphia, PA. I completed M.Sc. in Computer Science from the University of Louisiana at Lafayette, LA, USA. I also completed M.Sc. in Computer Engineering from King Fahd University of Petroleum and Minerals, Saudi Arabia. I completed B.Sc. in Computer Science from the Islamic University of Technology, IUT, Dhaka, Bangladesh.
